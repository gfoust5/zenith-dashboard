Daily KPA
System

Key Performance Actions — Open this before you start. Close the loop before you finish.

🌅
Opening Day ~20–30 MIN
01 Check Google Calendar ORIENT

Before touching anything else, know exactly what today looks like. Calls, check-ins, deadlines — orient yourself to the day's architecture first so everything else gets filtered through it.

Pro tip: If you find yourself context-switching mid-morning because you forgot a call, your opening routine failed. The calendar is your anchor.
02 Review Yesterday's Loose Ends CONTINUITY

Scan for anything unfinished, unanswered, or dropped from yesterday. A 2-minute scan here prevents things falling through cracks for days. Clients notice when they're forgotten — even once.

Check your notes from the previous closing day log. That's why you write them.
03 Set Your #1 Priority for the Day FOCUS

Name the one non-negotiable outcome for today. Not a task — an outcome. "Send proposal to Jordan" not "work on proposals." Write it somewhere visible. Everything else is secondary to this.

If you get to the end of a day and this one thing got done, the day was a win regardless of what else happened.
04 Check Revenue Dashboard NUMBERS

Know where you stand on your monthly revenue goal before the day starts. Are you ahead? Behind? This shapes your urgency. Business owners who don't look at their numbers daily drift — and wonder why they miss targets.

This should take 60 seconds. If it takes longer, your dashboard isn't set up right yet.
05 Clear Out Emails INBOX

Process — don't just read. Reply, archive, or flag for follow-up. Inbox Zero isn't a myth, it's a discipline. An unprocessed email inbox is a to-do list written by everyone else.

Set a 10-min timer. If an email takes more than 2 minutes to action, flag it for a dedicated block later.
06 Clear Client Inbox RETENTION

Your clients are paying for access and results. Response time is a form of service delivery. Clear your client communication channels first thing so no one feels ignored or deprioritized.

Same-day response to active clients is a retention superpower most coaches undervalue until they lose someone over it.
07 Check Typeform for New Applications PIPELINE

New applications are warm leads. The faster you follow up, the higher your close rate. Every hour you wait, their excitement cools and their options broaden. Check this daily without exception.

Speed to lead is one of the highest-leverage sales activities in your business. Treat a new application like a hand raised in a room.
08 Check Daily Programs Due DELIVERY

Review any client programs that need updating, adjusting, or delivering today. This is the core of your service. Proactively updating programs before clients ask signals world-class coaching and builds deep trust.

Clients who feel coached — not just subscribed — stay longer and refer more. Program updates are a touch point, not just a task.
09 Review Pending Proposals & Trial Clients CONVERSION

People on the fence need timely touches or they go cold and sign with someone else. Know exactly who is in "deciding" mode today and make deliberate contact. One follow-up email can be worth thousands.

Build a simple "pending" list in your CRM. If a proposal is 3+ days old with no reply, it needs a nudge today.
🌇
Closing Day ~20–25 MIN
01 Update CRM SYSTEMS

Log every client interaction, sales conversation, and status change from today. Your CRM is only as powerful as its data. If it's out of date, you're flying blind — and you'll eventually drop a ball that costs you a client or a deal.

A CRM updated daily takes 5 minutes. A CRM updated weekly takes 45 and is always missing something.
02 Update Lead Tracker PIPELINE

Move leads through stages, note where conversations stand, and flag anyone who needs follow-up tomorrow. Your pipeline is a living thing — leads that don't get moved forward either close or die.

If you can't tell at a glance how many leads are at each stage of your pipeline, your tracker isn't detailed enough.
03 Send Outstanding Follow-Ups SALES

Don't let the day end with unanswered leads, unacknowledged check-ins, or promises you made verbally but haven't confirmed in writing. Follow-up is where most revenue is made and most coaches are weakest.

"The fortune is in the follow-up" is a cliché because it's true. Most people stop at one touch. Most deals close on touch 4–7.
04 Clear IG DM Inbox SOCIAL

Instagram DMs are a direct sales and retention channel. Leaving them unread signals poor responsiveness to prospects and clients alike. Batch-process them at close of day so they don't interrupt your deep work blocks.

Every DM is a touch point. Even a quick reply keeps the relationship warm and the algorithm happy.
05 Clear Client Telegram Inbox RETENTION

End every day with a clean Telegram inbox. Clients who message their coach and don't hear back by end of day start to question the value of the investment. Consistency here compounds into deep loyalty.

Even "Got it, I'll review this tomorrow morning!" is better than silence. Acknowledgment is service.
06 Review Cash Flow & Invoices Due FINANCE

Flag anything overdue or coming up in the next 7 days. Chasing money reactively is stressful and unprofessional. Knowing your cash position proactively means you can act, not scramble.

A failed payment left unaddressed for 48 hours is a client relationship at risk. Catch it early and handle it gracefully.
07 Log Wins & Blockers GROWTH

Write 1–3 sentences: what moved forward today, what didn't, and why. This isn't journaling for therapy — it's an operational log. Over 90 days, patterns emerge that will tell you exactly where to fix your business.

The best operators review their own logs quarterly. You'll spot recurring blockers you'd never catch in the day-to-day fog.
08 Set Tomorrow's Top 3 Priorities PLANNING

Write three specific outcomes for tomorrow — not a task list, outcomes. Do this tonight so tomorrow morning you open with clarity, not chaos. Morning willpower is too valuable to spend on figuring out what to do.

Leaders who plan the next day the evening before consistently outperform those who plan in the morning. Your rested brain plans better than your just-woke-up brain.

Weekly Zoom-Out FRI OR SUN · 30 MIN
01 Review Weekly Revenue vs Target NUMBERS

Where did you land versus your weekly revenue target? Are you on pace for the month? Trend this weekly and you'll develop an intuition for your business that no dashboard can give you.

If you're consistently missing by 20–30%, the target isn't the problem — the activity is. Dig into which KPAs got skipped.
02 Review Client Retention & Satisfaction RETENTION

Who's been quiet this week? Who's struggling? Who is a flight risk? Scan your active roster with fresh eyes once a week. Proactive outreach to a at-risk client costs you 10 minutes. Losing them costs you months of revenue.

A client who cancels rarely gives you the real reason. Check in before they check out.
03 Review Pipeline Health SALES

How many leads entered this week? How many calls booked? How many conversions? If your pipeline is thin, you have a lead generation problem. If calls aren't converting, you have a sales problem. Know which one it is.

Most coaches confuse a sales problem with a lead problem and vice versa. The weekly pipeline review is how you stay clear.
04 Did Your Time Match Your Priorities? AUDIT

This is the most uncomfortable question and the most important one. Look back at where your time actually went this week versus where you intended it to go. Most business owners are busy but not productive. This question separates the two.

Busy ≠ productive. Revenue-generating activity and operational busywork feel the same in the moment. Only the weekly audit reveals the difference.
05 Identify One System to Improve OPERATIONS

Every week, name one thing in your operations that was clunky, slow, or broke down. Then decide: document it, delegate it, or automate it. This is how you build a real business instead of just a job with your name on it.

One system improvement per week = 50 improvements per year. Compounded over time, this is the difference between a $10K/mo business and a $50K/mo one.

⚡ The Rule That Ties It All Together

These KPAs only work if they're non-negotiable. Not "most days." Not "when I have time." Every operator who is consistently scaling has one thing in common: they do the boring, unsexy daily work with the same intensity they bring to the big moments. The open and close routines ARE the business. Everything else is just execution.
